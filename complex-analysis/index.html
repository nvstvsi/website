Complex Analysis

Functions of a Complex Variable

This subject develops the beautiful theory of functions of a complex variable. Analytic functions have remarkable properties—differentiability implies infinite differentiability, and local behavior determines global behavior through analytic continuation.

Planned Chapters

  • 1. The Complex Plane — ℂ, topology, extended plane, Möbius transformations
  • 2. Analytic Functions — Cauchy-Riemann equations, elementary functions
  • 3. Power Series — Radius of convergence, analytic functions as power series
  • 4. Complex Integration — Riemann-Stieltjes integrals, contour integrals
  • 5. Cauchy's Theorem — Goursat's lemma, Cauchy integral formula, consequences
  • 6. Singularities — Classification, Laurent series, meromorphic functions
  • 7. Residue Calculus — Residue theorem, evaluation of real integrals
  • 8. Maximum Principles — Maximum modulus, Phragmén-Lindelöf
