About This Project

These notes represent a personal endeavor to compile, organize, and clarify the material I have learned in mathematical analysis—from the foundations of the real line through advanced topics in measure theory and geometric measure theory.

Why This Project?

Analysis is a vast and interconnected subject. Textbooks tend to focus on specific areas, leaving the reader to piece together how different parts of analysis relate to one another. I wanted to create a unified reference that:

  • Emphasizes clarity and rigor without sacrificing readability
  • Highlights connections between different areas of analysis
  • Provides multiple learning paths depending on the reader's goals
  • Serves as both a learning resource and a reference

Structure

The notes are organized into four main subjects, each building on the previous:

  • Analysis I covers the real line and single-variable calculus with full rigor
  • Analysis II introduces topology, functional analysis, and differentiation in Banach spaces
  • Complex Analysis develops the theory of analytic functions
  • Analysis III covers measure theory, Lebesgue integration, and fine properties of functions, culminating in material needed for geometric measure theory
— C. Nastasi, 2024
